Science

Physics

  • Mechanics:

    Study of motion, forces, and how objects interact.

  • Optics:

    Study of light, reflection, refraction, and optical instruments.

  • Thermodynamics:

    Study of heat, temperature, energy transfer, and laws governing them.

  • Electromagnetism:

    Study of electric and magnetic fields, charges, currents, and electromagnetic waves.

  • Quantum Mechanics:

    Study of particles at atomic scales, probability-based behavior, and energy quantization.

Chemistry

  • Organic Chemistry:

    Study of carbon-based compounds and their reactions.

  • Inorganic Chemistry:

    Focuses on compounds not based on carbon, including metals, minerals, and salts.

  • Physical Chemistry:

    Examines the physical properties of molecules and the forces that act on them.

  • Analytical Chemistry:

    Techniques and methods to identify and quantify chemical substances.

  • Biochemistry:

    Study of chemical processes in living organisms.

Math

  • Algebra:

    Study of symbols and rules for manipulating them to solve equations.

  • Geometry:

    Deals with shapes, sizes, and properties of space and figures.

  • Calculus:

    Focuses on change and motion, using derivatives and integrals.

  • Statistics:

    Analysis and interpretation of data using probability and trends.

  • Number Theory:

    Study of integers and the relationships between them.
